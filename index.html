Explainable Artificial Intelligence over Knowledge Graph: from Reinforcement Learning to Generative Modeling

21 - 25 July 2025
Boise State University, Computer Science Department

Course Aims and Scope

Intelligent systems shape many daily decisions, but their reasoning is often unclear. Among them, Recommender Systems personalize content but need transparency to build trust. This course explores how Knowledge Graphs (KGs) can enhance explainability by structuring knowledge and supporting reasoning. Students will integrate KGs with Reinforcement Learning and Generative Models , using tools like HuggingFace and Hopewise in hands-on sessions to build explainable recommender solutions.

What you will learn

  • Enhance model explainability by integrating Knowledge Graphs into intelligent systems
  • Use Large Language Models and Reinforcement Learning to provide explainable recommendations
  • Understand how Transformers and Large Language Models work, including their core architecture and mechanisms
  • Train and evaluate recommender systems with HuggingFace and Hopewise libraries

Who is it for

This course is designed for graduate students and advanced undergraduates in computer science, artificial intelligence, or data science who are interested in building intelligent systems that are not only effective but also transparent and explainable.

Date Day Sessions
21/07 Day 1: Foundation of Explainable AI Welcome and Course Overview
  • Introduction to the course structure, objectives, and key topics.
Core Concepts: Explainability and Knowledge Grounding
  • We will explore the fundamental principles of explainable AI, with a focus on leveraging structured knowledge for reasoning and transparency. This session sets the theoretical groundwork for understanding how explanations can be derived from knowledge-driven models.
Hands-on Session: Interaction Data and Knowledge Graphs
  • An introduction to two publicly available datasets: MovieLens-1M (ML1M) and COCO. These datasets differ in domain, size, and sparsity, providing a diverse testing ground.
  • Loading and exploring knowledge graphs and necessary preprocessing steps.
  • Applying various knowledge embedding techniques.
22/07 Day 2: Reinforcement Knowledge Graph Reasoning Welcome & Recap of Day 1

Knowledge Graph Reasoning in Personalized Recommendations
  • Embedding-based and path-based approaches.
  • Focus on PGPR and CAFE models.
Hands-on Session: PGPR & CAFE models
  • Implementing PGPR and CAFE using the Hopewise library.
  • Training and evaluation of models.
23/07 Day 3: Foundations of Generative Models Welcome & Recap of Day 2

Introduction to Transformer Architecture
  • We provide a foundational overview of the Transformer architecture, as introduced in the paper ‚ÄúAttention is All You Need‚Äù. Key components such as self-attention, positional encoding, and multi-head attention mechanisms will be explained to ground participants in the core principles behind modern generative models.
Hands-on Session: Hugging Face Transformers
  • Overview of the library‚Äôs structure and key components.
  • Deep dive into tokenization, with a comparison of different tokenizers and their impact on model input representation.
  • Exploration of the AutoModelForSeq2SeqLM architecture, showcasing how to load and utilize pre-trained models for sequence-to-sequence language modeling tasks.
24/07 Day 4: Explainable Generative Reasoning Welcome & Recap of Day 3

Language Models for Explainable Recommendations
  • We provide a overview on the paradigms of language modeling (i.e., causal, masked, and text-to-text) and their applications, focusing on recommendation.
  • We explore innovative integrations of KGs and language models to enhance explainability. Special focus will be given to PLM model (Path Language Modeling over Knowledge Graphs for Explainable Recommendation).
Hands-on Session: PLM Model
  • Learning how to construct training data by sampling reasoning paths from knowledge graphs.
  • Using path-languale modeling to generate top-k recommendations along with natural language explanations.
25/07 Day 5: Faithful Generative Reasoning Welcome & Recap of Day 4

Faithfulness in Generative Reasoning
  • This session explores the critical concept of faithfulness ‚Äîensuring that generated recommendations and their explanations accurately reflect the underlying knowledge graph.
  • Addressing the challenge of hallucination, where generated explanations may diverge from the underlying KG, and exploring mitigation strategies.
Hands-on Session: PEARLM Model
  • Implementing PEARLM with Hugging Face and Hopewise libraries.
  • Training and evaluation.
Final Discussion & Conclusions
  • Reflection and future directions.

Francesca Maridina Malloci is Assistant Professor at the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science of the University of Cagliari (Italy). Her research focuses on responsible artificial intelligence, with attention to decision-making systems, such as recommender systems, for multi-stakeholder contexts. She has co-authored papers in international journals and conferences. Francesca has chaired workshops and tutorials, including those on knowledge discovery at ACM UMAP,ECML-PKDD, and AIED, on explainable AI at ECIR, and on bias and fairness at SIGIR. She also serves as Associate Editor for Neural Processing Letters (Springer) and Information Processing in Agriculture (Elsevier).
